Trauma Response Identifier

A gentle look at how you protect yourself under stress — and how to come back to calm.

🤍 A gentle word before you begin

This is a space for self-understanding, not a diagnosis. Nothing here is medical or therapeutic advice. If any of this stirs something heavy, please know that reaching out to a licensed therapist or counselor is a wise and brave step — not a weakness. You are worth that kind of care, sister.

1

How do you protect yourself?

When we feel unsafe or overwhelmed, our bodies reach for an old way of coping. None of these are wrong — they kept you safe. Read each with kindness and gently select the one(s) you most relate to.

✓ Fight · stand my ground Stress can show up as irritation, control, or pushing back — your protector says "I'll meet this head-on."
✓ Flight · keep moving Stress can show up as restlessness, over-busyness, or the urge to escape — your protector says "let's get out of here."
✓ Freeze · go still Stress can show up as numbness, shutting down, or feeling stuck — your protector says "if I'm still, this will pass."
✓ Fawn · keep the peace Stress can show up as people-pleasing, over-apologizing, or losing yourself in others' needs — your protector says "if I keep everyone happy, I'll be safe."
2

A few gentle prompts

No right answers — just notice what's true. These can help you sense your pattern.

  • ?When something hard happens, what does my body want to do first — fight back, leave, freeze, or smooth it over?
  • ?After a tense moment, do I feel angry, anxious, numb, or guilty?
  • ?What was rewarded or kept me safe when I was younger?
  • ?Where in my body do I feel stress — my chest, jaw, stomach, shoulders?
  • ?What would it feel like to let myself be safe right now?
