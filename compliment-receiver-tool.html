Why it's hard to receive — and how to let it land

When someone praises you, do you rush to deflect? "Oh, this old thing." "It was nothing." We brush off compliments to seem humble, or because deep down a kind word feels unsafe to believe. But every deflection quietly tells your heart the good isn't true.

Here's the practice, sister: when a compliment comes, pause. Breathe. Then simply say —

"Thank you. That means a lot."

No minimizing. No deflecting. Just let it land. Receiving isn't pride — it's agreeing with what's already true about you.
